Tal Barahi Temple

The Tal Barahi Temple, also known as the Lake Temple or Varahi Temple, is a two-story pagoda temple located on a small island in Pokhara's Phewa Lake. Dedicated to Goddess Varahi, an avatar of Goddess Durga, this temple attracts both Hindu and Buddhist devotees.

The temple, built by King Kulmandhan Shah, features traditional Nepali pagoda architecture. Accessible only by boat, it is a serene and spiritual retreat. Thousands of devotees visit during Durga Puja and on Saturdays to offer prayers and seek blessings.

Set against the stunning backdrop of the Annapurna mountain range, the temple is a must-visit for pilgrims, heritage enthusiasts, and nature lovers alike. A visit to the Tal Barahi Temple offers a glimpse into Nepali culture, art, and spirituality.
